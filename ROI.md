# ROI — the buy-in math

A one-pager for the non-technical stakeholder. Numbers are illustrative — swap in real ones.

## The trade
Today the weekly brief (and the other workflows) is hand-assembled by one person. Beacon
replaces that with an always-on, grounded run.

| | Today (manual) | Beacon (v0) |
|---|---|---|
| Time / week | ~2–3 analyst hours assembling + fact-checking | ~0 (scheduled) |
| Reliability | sleeps with the laptop | always-on |
| Trust | *"do I believe this number?"* | every line cites a real source; bad briefs are held |
| Cost | the analyst's time | ~$0.01–0.05 per run (one model call) |

## The math (illustrative)
- **Saved:** ~2.5 hrs/week × $75/hr × 52 ≈ **$9,750/yr** of analyst time — for *one* workflow.
- **Cost:** ~1 run/week × ~$0.03 + n8n.cloud (free–$20/mo) ≈ **< $250/yr**.
- **Net:** roughly **40× return** on the first workflow — *before* the platform (v1) makes the
  2nd and 3rd nearly free.

## Why it compounds
The expensive part (connectors, memory, the trust gate) is built once in v0–v1; each new
workflow after that rents it, so ROI rises with every workflow. The risk-reduction — no silent
failures, no ungrounded or speculative claim reaching a customer — isn't in the table, but it's
the reason leadership can act on the output at all.
