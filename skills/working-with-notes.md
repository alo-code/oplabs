# Skill: working with notes.md

`notes.md` is a case-study deliverable: a raw log of how the work was done
with AI. It is also just good practice. These are the rules.

## What goes in
- The decision and the *why*, especially the ones you might second-guess later
  (e.g. "TS-only despite the JD listing Python/Go, because…").
- Prompts that mattered, and the corrections you had to make to the AI's
  output. The corrections are the most interesting part — they show judgment.
- Dead ends. The approach you tried and abandoned, and why. This is signal,
  not noise — it shows the path wasn't obvious.
- Commands run + real output (or a pointer to the `evidence/` capture).
- Open questions to yourself, as a checklist.

## Rules
- **Append-only, newest at the bottom, timestamped/dated.**
- **Do not clean it up.** Typos, false starts, and "wait, that was wrong" are
  the point. A polished log is a fake log.
- **Don't paste secrets.** Real keys never go here (or anywhere but `.env`).
- **Don't turn it into the README.** README/docs are the cleaned story;
  `notes.md` is the unedited one. They serve different readers.

## Why it matters here
The role is "turn a prototype into something the org trusts." Trust comes from
being able to see how a thing was built and judged — not just the polished
result. `notes.md` is that audit trail for the build process itself, the same
way `evidence/` is the audit trail for the running system.
